Executive Function
Executive function is your brain‚Äôs management system‚Äîit helps you plan, focus, and juggle multiple tasks (Barkley, 2012). Differences in executive function explain why some people thrive with structure while others need flexibility.

Motivation Style
Motivation theory (Deci & Ryan, 1985) distinguishes between intrinsic motivation‚Äîdriven by meaning or curiosity‚Äîand extrinsic motivation, fueled by rewards, deadlines, or recognition. Neither is ‚Äúbetter‚Äù‚Äîthey just require different strategies to sustain.

Cognitive Load & Focus
Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller, 1988) shows that our brains have limited working memory. Those with broad focus excel at creative synthesis but can get overwhelmed by too many open loops; narrow-focus types thrive with deep work but may struggle to pivot quickly.
